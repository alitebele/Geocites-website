A Royal Canadian Air Cadet Site on the Web

Here is some interesting information.

Royal Canadian Air Cadets was formed in 1942 to train young men in avaition in preparation for the war. After the war, the aim was changed to these three:

  1. To promote in youth the attributes of citizenship and leadership qualities.

  2. To promote physical fitness.

  3. To stimulate in youth an interest in the Air element of the Canadian forces.

Royal Canadian Air Cadets are not a part of the Military. We are supported by DND(Department of National Defense) and the Air Cadet League of Canada. DND supplies Training and Supplies to the squadrons and they also train the officers which are in charge of the squadrons. Air Cadet League of Canada supply the Administration part of the program. Also the League provides scholarships for the Air Cadets.

In 1976, girls were permitted to enter Cadets. A squadron is a group of Air Cadets. I am currently part of 521 Aurora Squadron in Mission, B.C.. Canada has around 400 squadrons.

The Air Cadet program consists of a five year program:

  • The first year is used to introduce the cadet to the Air Cadet program. At the end of the first year cadets are allowed to go to a summer camp. the usual camp is Basic. A two weel summer camp which prepares the cadet for the next level of training.
  • In the second year the cadet is introduced to more aviation topics, Leadership and Citzenship. After this year cadets have a varity of summer camps which they can apply for.
  • The Third year and Fourth year is spent in advanced Aviation topics and Leadership. When cadets reach this level they are learning how to teach and have a leadership role in the squadron. In this year Cadets are able to apply for scholarship summer camps.
  • The Fifth year is used to prepare cadets to take command of the squadron. The training is geared towards the Warrent Officer positions in the squadron. The cadets are instructing and leading. Apply for advanced summer camps (Flying Scholarships)
  • The sixth and seventh year is instructing cadets and leading the squadron as Squadron Commander, Deputy-Commander or Flight Commanders. These cadets apply for staff positions at summer camps.
